# 👆 FINGER FLICK MECHANIC
## Physical Impact System for Zero Assembly

**Status:** Integrated into dice-based combat system
**Purpose:** Add physical interaction and spectacle while maintaining strategic gameplay

---

## 🎯 OVERVIEW

The **Finger Flick** mechanic combines the best of both worlds:
- **Strategic dice-based combat** (line of sight, cover, modifiers)
- **Physical interaction** (flicking mechs, knockback, terrain destruction)

When your attack hits (after rolling dice), you perform a Finger Flick on the target mech to simulate the physical impact of the shot. This creates exciting moments where mechs can be knocked back, fall over, or collide with terrain.

---

## 📋 HOW IT WORKS

### Basic Procedure

1. **Roll to hit** (2D6, need 7+)
2. **If you hit:**
   - Place HP tokens at target's feet (weapon's DP value)
   - **Perform Finger Flick(s)** on the target mech

### How to Finger Flick

1. **Position:** Place your finger against the target mech's torso or legs
2. **Flick:** Flick your finger forward with a quick, controlled motion
3. **Result:** The mech may slide back, spin, or fall over

### Number of Flicks

| Weapon Damage | Flicks | Examples |
|---------------|--------|----------|
| **1 DP** | 1 Flick | G-36 Micro-Rifle, Paralyzer EMP Beam |
| **2 DP** | 1 Flick | M10 Breacher Shotgun, Lancer Railgun |
| **3 DP** | 2 Flicks | Bunker Pile Driver, Storm Shadow Pod |
| **Multi-Shot** | 1 Flick per hit | Twin-Link Rifle (2 hits = 2 flicks) |

**Special Weapons:**
- **Avalanche Cannon (2 DP):** 2 Flicks (extra knockback)
- **Sonic Repulsor (0 DP):** 3 Flicks (pure knockback weapon)

---

## 💥 FLICK RESULTS

### Possible Outcomes

**1. Mech Slides Back**
- No additional effect
- Just visual/cinematic
- Mech stays standing

**2. Mech Falls Over**
- Target must use REPOSITION (free action) to stand up
- No additional damage
- Vulnerable position (can't move until standing)

**3. Mech Falls Off Ledge (>5cm)**
- Target takes **+1 DP** fall damage
- Must use REPOSITION to stand up
- Place additional HP token at feet

**4. Mech Collides with Terrain**
- If terrain falls on mech: **+1 DP**
- Terrain piece is knocked over/destroyed
- Creates new ground-level cover

**5. Mech Pushed into Hazard**
- If mech slides into hazard marker: **+1 DP**
- Tactical positioning matters!

## 🎮 TACTICAL IMPLICATIONS

### Offensive Tactics

**1. Edge Control**
- Position enemies near ledges
- Flick them off for +1 DP fall damage
- Denies them high ground advantage

**2. Terrain Destruction**
- Flick enemies into unstable terrain
- Collapsing terrain deals +1 DP
- Removes enemy cover

**3. Hazard Pushing**
- Use mines/flame markers strategically
- Flick enemies into hazards for +1 DP
- Combine with area denial weapons

**4. Knockback Chains**
- Multiple flicks can push mechs far
- Sonic Repulsor (3 flicks) = maximum distance
- Push enemies out of objective zones

### Defensive Tactics

**1. Stable Positioning**
- Stand away from ledges
- Avoid unstable terrain
- Wide stance = harder to knock over

**2. Low Center of Gravity**
- Leg-heavy builds harder to topple
- Top-heavy builds fall easier
- Build design matters!

**3. Terrain Anchoring**
- Position near solid terrain
- Use walls to block knockback
- Corner positions limit flick angles

**Tri-Barrel Spread Cannon**

- **Shots:** 3 shots

**Starstreak Missile Rack**
- **Damage:** 1 DP per hit
- **Shots:** 3 shots
- **Flicks:** 1 flick per successful hit (0-3 flicks total)

## 🏗️ TERRAIN INTERACTION

### Flicking Buildings

**When to Flick Buildings:**
- Bunker Pile Driver special ability
- Optional: Any weapon can target buildings (GM discretion)

**Building Flick Procedure:**
1. Declare you're targeting a building
2. Roll to hit (if required)
3. Perform Finger Flick on the building piece
4. If building falls over, it's destroyed
5. Leave fallen pieces as ground-level cover

**Tactical Uses:**
- Destroy enemy cover
- Create new cover positions
- Block movement paths
- Cause collateral damage to nearby mechs

### Unstable Terrain

**Building Stability:**
- Stacked buildings are less stable
- Tall buildings easier to knock over
- Connected buildings support each other

**Strategic Considerations:**
- Build stable terrain (wide bases)
- Or build unstable terrain (easy to destroy)
- Terrain destruction is part of the game!

## 🎲 COMBINING DICE + FLICKS

### The Full Combat Sequence

**Example: Shooting with G-36 Micro-Rifle (1 DP, Medium range)**

1. **Declare:** "I'm shooting at the enemy mech with my rifle"
2. **Check LoS:** Can I see the target? Yes
3. **Measure Range:** 20cm = Medium range
4. **Check Cover:** Target is in Partial Cover (-1)
5. **Roll:** 2D6 = 8 (need 8+ with cover penalty)
6. **Hit!** 
7. **Apply Damage:** Place 1 HP token at target's feet
8. **Finger Flick:** Perform 1 flick on the target
9. **Result:** Target slides back 5cm and falls over!
10. **Bonus:** Target must use REPOSITION to stand up

**Total Effect:**
- 1 DP damage (strategic)
- Knockback + fall (physical/cinematic)
- Target loses free action standing up (tactical)

## 🎯 DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

### Why Finger Flicks Work

**1. Best of Both Worlds**
- Dice provide strategic depth and balance
- Flicks provide physical interaction and excitement
- Neither system dominates the other

**2. Predictable + Unpredictable**
- Dice rolls are predictable (probability)
- Flick results are unpredictable (physics)
- Creates tension and memorable moments

**3. Skill Expression**
- Dice reward tactical positioning
- Flicks reward build design (stability)
- Both matter for success

**4. Spectacle**
- Watching mechs get knocked around is fun
- Terrain destruction is satisfying
- Physical interaction engages players

**5. Safety**
- No projectiles flying across the room
- Controlled finger flicks are safe
- Still maintains physical component

## 📊 BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS

### Flick Power vs. Damage

**Light Weapons (1 DP, 1 Flick):**
- Low damage, low knockback
- Reliable but not spectacular
- Good for consistent pressure

**Medium Weapons (2 DP, 1 Flick):**
- Balanced damage and knockback
- Most common weapon type
- Versatile tactical options

**Heavy Weapons (3 DP, 2 Flicks):**
- High damage, high knockback
- Spectacular but rare
- Game-changing moments

**Knockback Specialists (0-2 DP, 2-3 Flicks):**
- Low/no damage, maximum knockback
- Tactical positioning tools
- Combo with terrain/hazards

### Build Stability

**Stable Builds:**
- Wide leg stance
- Low center of gravity
- Heavy bottom, light top
- **Advantage:** Hard to knock over
- **Disadvantage:** May be slower or less armed

**Unstable Builds:**
- Narrow leg stance
- High center of gravity
- Top-heavy design
- **Advantage:** More weapons/armor
- **Disadvantage:** Easy to knock over

**Trade-off:** Firepower vs. Stability

## 🔍 ADVANCED TACTICS

### Flick Angles

**Directional Control:**
- Flick from different angles for different results
- Flick toward ledges for fall damage
- Flick toward hazards for bonus damage
- Flick toward walls to limit knockback

**Positioning Matters:**
- Your mech's position determines flick angle
- Flanking gives better flick angles
- Frontal flicks push enemy back (safe)
- Side flicks can spin enemy (disorienting)

### Combo Plays

**1. Mine + Knockback**
- Place mines with Skorpion Mine Dispenser
- Shoot enemy with knockback weapon
- Flick enemy into mines
- Damage + mine damage

**2. Ledge + Heavy Weapon**
- Maneuver enemy near ledge
- Hit with 3 DP weapon (2 flicks)
- High chance of fall
- Damage + fall damage

**3. Multi-Shot + Unstable Terrain**
- Target enemy near unstable building
- Fire multi-shot weapon
- Multiple flicks increase chance of terrain collapse
- Damage + collateral damage

### Counter-Play

**Against Knockback:**
- Position away from ledges
- Use solid terrain as backstop
- Build stable mechs (wide stance)
- Destroy enemy knockback weapons first

**Against Terrain Destruction:**
- Don't rely on single cover piece
- Spread out (avoid collateral)
- Use low, stable cover
- Rebuild cover with fallen pieces

## 📝 RULES CLARIFICATIONS

### Common Questions

**Q: Can I choose the direction of the flick?**
A: Yes, within reason. Flick from your mech's position toward the target. You control the angle.

**Q: What if the mech doesn't move from the flick?**
A: That's fine! Heavy/stable mechs may not move much. The flick still happened.

**Q: Can I flick harder for more knockback?**
A: No. Use a controlled, consistent flick. The number of flicks matters, not the force.

**Q: What if the mech falls off the table?**
A: Treat it as falling off a >5cm ledge. +1 DP, place mech at table edge.

**Q: Can I flick buildings without a weapon?**
A: No, unless you have Bunker Pile Driver or GM allows it as a house rule.

**Q: Do artillery weapons (ignore cover) still flick?**
A: Yes! Even indirect fire creates impact. Flick normally.

**Q: What if multiple flicks knock the mech in different directions?**
A: Perform flicks sequentially. Each flick affects the mech's new position.

**Q: Can I flick my own mech?**
A: No. Flicks only happen when you hit an enemy.

- Modifiers for build design

- Must deal damage to destroy

- 1 flick = up to 5cm
