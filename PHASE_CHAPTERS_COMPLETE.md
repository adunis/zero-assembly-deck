## 📖 NEW CHAPTERS ADDED

### **PHASE 1: TERRAIN BUILDING**
**ID:** `phase_1`

**Content:**
- **Objective:** Create a dynamic battlefield with cover, elevation, and tactical opportunities
- **Procedure:** 
  - Gather terrain pieces
  - Determine first player (coin flip)
  - Alternate placement
  - Spacing rules: 15cm from other terrain and board edges
  - Continue until complete
- **Strategic Considerations:**
  - Cover placement for firing positions
  - Elevation for tactical advantages
  - Firing lanes vs close-quarters areas
  - Think ahead for objectives
  - Consider mobility types (wheels, spiders, etc.)
- **Time:** 5-10 minutes

---

### **PHASE 2: SCAVENGING (BUILD PHASE)**
**ID:** `phase_2`

**Content:**
- **Objective:** Assemble your mech(s) from available parts in 10 minutes
- **Procedure:**
  - Set 10-minute timer
  - Choose frame count (1 or 2 mechs)
  - Scavenge parts from shared pool
  - Build requirements (1 Core + 1 Top per frame)
  - Timer expires → stop building
  - Stability check (30 seconds to fix if fallen)
- **Frame Options:**
  - 1 Solo Mech: 4 HP, 8 slots, concentrated firepower
  - 2 Solo Mechs: 4 HP each, 8 slots each, board control
  - 1 Duo Mech: 8 HP, 16 slots, massive firepower
- **Color Synergy:**
  - 1-3 colors (SYNCHED) = 1 Pilot Perk
  - 4+ colors (UNSYNCHED) = 0 Perks
- **Veteran Option:** Skip or participate in scavenging
- **Build Tips:**
  - Start with Core + Top
  - Add legs early for stability testing
  - Balance weapons to prevent tipping
  - Test stability during building
  - Hybrid locomotion for flexibility
  - Armor placement for protection
- **Scavenging Pile:** Unused parts go to pile for Equipment cubes
- **Time:** Exactly 10 minutes

---

### **PHASE 3: SCENARIO PREPARATION**
**ID:** `phase_3`

**Content:**
- **Objective:** Set up the chosen scenario by placing Objectives, Resources, and Equipment
- **Procedure:**
  - Choose scenario (Deathmatch, CTF, King of the Hill, etc.)
  - Read scenario rules
  - Place Objectives per scenario instructions
  - Alternate placing Resources (3-5 tokens on terrain)
  - Alternate placing Equipment (2-3 cubes on terrain)
- **Objectives:**
  - Scenario-specific markers (flags, control points, terminals)
  - Placement specified by scenario
  - Interaction varies by scenario
  - VP value defined in scenario rules
- **Resources:**
  - Battlefield salvage (ammo, parts, medical supplies)
  - Place on terrain (not ground)
  - SCAVENGE action → Gain 1 HP
  - Strategy: Place in contested areas
- **Equipment:**
  - Advanced salvage (experimental weapons, prototypes)
  - Place on terrain (not ground)
  - SCAVENGE action → Random part from scavenging pile
  - **ONLY way to modify mech during game**
  - Strategy: High risk, high reward
- **Placement Strategy:**
  - Contested zones create conflict
  - High ground rewards climbers
  - Balance cover vs exposure
  - Place near objectives for multi-tasking
- **Time:** 3-5 minutes

---

### **PHASE 4: DEPLOYMENT**
**ID:** `phase_4`

**Content:**
- **Objective:** Place your mech(s) on the battlefield in your deployment zone
- **Procedure:**
  - Define deployment zones (corners or edges)
  - Determine deployment order (coin flip)
    - Loser picks zone first
    - Winner takes remaining zone, deploys last
  - Place mechs fully within zone
  - Mechs can face any direction
  - Mechs must be standing
  - Final check for legality and stability
- **Deployment Zones:**
  - Opposite Corners: 30cm x 30cm corner each
  - Opposite Edges: 15cm deep strip along edges
  - Scenario-Specific: Custom zones per scenario
- **Deployment Strategy:**
  - Face weapons toward expected positions
  - Spread out for board control or group for support
  - Deploy behind cover to reduce exposure
  - Position wheeled mechs on flat ground
  - Long-range mechs deploy back, close-range forward
- **Deployment Order Advantage:**
  - Last to deploy can react to opponent's placement
  - Balanced by picking zone first
- **Multiple Mechs:**
  - Spread out for board control
  - Group up for concentrated firepower
  - Specialized roles (aggressive + support)
- **Time:** 2-3 minutes

### **PHASE 5: COMBAT**
**ID:** `phase_5`

**Content:**
- **Objective:** Fight for 4 Rounds, score Victory Points, destroy the enemy
- **Procedure:**
  - Determine first player (coin flip)
  - Round structure: Players alternate turns
  - Turn structure: 2 AP total per turn
  - Available actions:
    - MOVE (1 AP): Move one mech one ruler
    - ENGAGE (1 AP): Fire one weapon
    - SCAVENGE (1 AP): Pick up HP/Resource/Equipment
    - REPOSITION (1 AP): Rotate, re-pose, or stand up
  - Round ends after all players act
  - Start new round (4 rounds total)
  - Game ends after Round 4, count VP
- **Action Points (AP):**
  - 2 AP per turn TOTAL (not per mech)
  - Spend both on one mech OR split between mechs
  - Use same action twice (e.g., MOVE + MOVE)
- **Combat Flow:**
  - Declare action
  - Execute action
  - Resolve effects (damage, HP tokens, falling)
  - Repeat with remaining AP
  - End turn, pass to next player
- **Victory Conditions:**
  - **Instant Victory:** Destroy enemy Core (0 HP)
  - **Victory Points:** After Round 4, count VP:
    - Scenario objectives (varies)
    - 1 VP per enemy HP token on table
    - 5 VP per enemy Core destroyed
  - Highest VP wins
- **Damage & HP:**
  - Hit → Place HP tokens at feet (1 per DP)
  - Tokens become scrap (anyone can scavenge)
  - A.T. Shields absorb first (vanish, can't scavenge)
  - 0 HP → Destroyed
- **Tactical Considerations:**
  - Use terrain for cover and elevation
  - Scavenge HP tokens to heal
  - Don't forget scenario objectives
  - Risk scavenging Equipment for upgrades
  - Use Pilot Perk at right moment
  - Coordinate multi-mech actions
- **Round Timer (Optional):** 15 minutes per round
- **Game End:** After Round 4, count VP. Highest wins. Tie → Most HP wins. Still tied → Draw.
- **Sportsmanship:** Physics-based game, be gracious, re-shoot disputes, stand up bumped mechs
