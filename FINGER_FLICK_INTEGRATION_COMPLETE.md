## How It Works

### Basic Combat Flow

1. **Roll to hit** (2D6, need 7+ with modifiers)
2. **If you hit:**
   - Place HP tokens at target's feet (weapon's DP value)
   - **Perform Finger Flick(s)** on the target mech
3. **Flick results:**
   - Mech slides back (visual effect)
   - Mech falls over (must REPOSITION)
   - Mech falls off ledge >5cm (+1 DP)
   - Terrain collapses on mech (+1 DP)
   - Mech pushed into hazard (+1 DP)

### Example Combat

**Scenario:** Shooting with Avalanche Cannon (2 DP, 2 Flicks) at Medium range

1. Check line of sight ✓
2. Measure range: 25cm = Medium
3. Roll 2D6 = 9 (hit!)
4. Place 2 HP tokens at target's feet
5. **Perform 2 Finger Flicks:**
   - First flick: Target slides back 5cm
   - Second flick: Target falls over!
6. **Result:** 2 DP damage + target must use REPOSITION to stand

## Tactical Implications

### Offensive Tactics

**Edge Control**
- Position enemies near ledges
- Flick them off for +1 DP fall damage

**Terrain Destruction**
- Flick enemies into unstable terrain
- Collapsing terrain deals +1 DP

**Hazard Pushing**
- Flick enemies into mines/flames
- Bonus damage from hazards

**Knockback Chains**
- Multiple flicks push mechs far
- Sonic Repulsor (3 flicks) = maximum distance

### Defensive Tactics

**Stable Positioning**
- Stand away from ledges
- Avoid unstable terrain

**Build Design**
- Wide leg stance = harder to knock over
- Low center of gravity = more stable
- Trade-off: Stability vs. Firepower

**Terrain Anchoring**
- Position near solid terrain
- Use walls to block knockback
