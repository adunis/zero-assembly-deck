# RAMMING & TERRAIN INTERACTION RULES - UPDATED ✅

## Summary
Updated terrain interaction mechanics to allow direct ramming of enemy mechs. Players can now ram enemies during movement, but must follow the "hands-off" rule: only touch your own mech during movement.

---

## NEW MECHANICS

### 1. Ramming Enemy Mechs (ALLOWED!)
**Rule:** You CAN ram into enemy mechs during movement.

**How It Works:**
- Move your mech toward an enemy mech
- Your mech collides with the enemy
- Enemy gets pushed along your movement path
- If enemy falls over, they must use REPOSITION to stand up
- **Hands-Off Rule:** You can only touch YOUR OWN mech during movement

**Example:** You move your mech 15cm forward. An enemy is in your path. Your mech rams the enemy, pushing them backward. The enemy falls over. You only touched your own mech during this move.

---

### 2. Hands-Off Rule (CRITICAL!)
**Rule:** During your movement, you can only touch YOUR OWN mech.

**Why:** This prevents manipulation and keeps the game fair. Let physics handle the collision naturally.

**What You Can Do:**
- ✅ Touch your own mech to move it
- ✅ Push your mech into enemies
- ✅ Push your mech into terrain

**What You CANNOT Do:**
- ❌ Touch enemy mechs with your hands
- ❌ Manually position enemy mechs after collision
- ❌ Adjust enemy mech's final position

**Let Physics Decide:** Where the enemy mech ends up after collision is determined by the physical impact, not by manual placement.

---

### 3. Pushing Terrain
**Rule:** You can push terrain pieces with your mech as you move.

**How It Works:**
- Move your mech toward terrain
- Terrain moves along your ruler direction
- Terrain can collide with enemy mechs (indirect pushing)
- If terrain knocks over an enemy, they must use REPOSITION

**Example:** You push a building forward. The building collides with an enemy mech, knocking it over.

---

### 4. Climbing Restriction
**Rule:** If terrain moves or falls while you're trying to climb it, the climb fails.

**Scenarios:**
- **Stable terrain:** Climb succeeds
- **Moving terrain:** Climb fails, mech stays in starting position
- **Partial placement:** Applies to 2 legs on building + 2 legs on ground

---

## DETAILED RULES

### Ramming Rules
1. **Ramming is allowed** - You can ram enemy mechs during movement
2. **Hands-off rule** - Only touch your own mech
3. **Let physics decide** - Don't manually position enemies after collision
4. **Falling enemies** - If enemy falls over, they must REPOSITION
5. **No ram damage** - Ramming doesn't deal damage (unless enemy falls from height >5cm)

### Terrain Pushing Rules
1. **You can push terrain** - Move toward it, it moves with you
2. **Follow ruler direction** - Terrain moves along your movement path
3. **Indirect ramming** - Terrain can push enemies
4. **Hands-off applies** - Only touch your own mech, not terrain or enemies

### Climbing Rules
1. **Pure Legs only** - Only Pure Legs can climb (up to 5cm)
2. **Stable terrain required** - Terrain must not move during climb
3. **Partial placement** - Applies to partial placement (2 legs on building, 2 on ground)
4. **Climb fails if terrain moves** - If terrain shifts, climb fails, mech stays in place

### Falling Rules
1. **Fall from height >5cm** - Take 1 DP
2. **Pushed off ledge** - Take 1 DP (in addition to weapon damage)
3. **Terrain collapse** - Take 1 DP if terrain falls on you
4. **Ramming collision** - No damage from ram itself (unless falling from height)

## GAMEPLAY EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Direct Ram Attack
**Setup:** Your mech is 10cm from an enemy mech. Clear path between you.

**Action:** MOVE (1 AP) - Move 15cm forward toward the enemy.

**Execution:**
1. Place ruler from your mech toward enemy
2. Move your mech along the ruler (only touching your own mech)
3. Your mech collides with enemy mech
4. Enemy gets pushed backward by the impact
5. Enemy falls over
6. You did NOT touch the enemy mech with your hands

**Result:** Enemy must use REPOSITION next turn to stand up.

**Tactical Advantage:** You've disrupted the enemy without attacking, and they lose their free REPOSITION for positioning.

---

### Example 2: Terrain-Assisted Ram
**Setup:** Building is between you and enemy mech.

**Action:** MOVE (1 AP) - Push building toward enemy.

**Execution:**
1. Move your mech toward building
2. Building gets pushed forward (only touching your own mech)
3. Building collides with enemy mech
4. Enemy gets knocked over by building

**Result:** Enemy must use REPOSITION next turn.

**Tactical Advantage:** Indirect attack using terrain as a weapon.

---

### Example 3: Ram Off Ledge
**Setup:** Enemy mech is standing near a ledge (6cm high).

**Action:** MOVE (1 AP) - Ram enemy toward the ledge.

**Execution:**
1. Move your mech toward enemy
2. Ram enemy mech
3. Enemy gets pushed backward
4. Enemy falls off ledge (6cm > 5cm threshold)

**Result:** Enemy takes 1 DP from fall damage AND must use REPOSITION.

**Tactical Advantage:** Ram + fall damage combo!

---

### Example 4: Failed Hands-Off Rule
**Setup:** You ram an enemy mech.

**Wrong Execution:**
1. Move your mech toward enemy
2. Ram enemy mech
3. ❌ You touch the enemy mech with your hands to position it
4. ❌ You manually place the enemy where you want

**Penalty:** Illegal move. Redo the movement without touching the enemy.

**Correct Execution:**
1. Move your mech toward enemy
2. Ram enemy mech
3. ✅ Let the enemy mech fall naturally from the impact
4. ✅ Don't touch the enemy mech at all

**Result:** Legal move. Enemy ends up wherever physics takes them.

## STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

### Offensive Uses
1. **Direct Ramming** - Ram enemies to knock them over
2. **Ledge Rams** - Push enemies off high ground for fall damage
3. **Terrain Rams** - Push terrain into enemies
4. **Combo Attacks** - Ram + Finger Flick for double disruption

### Defensive Uses
1. **Push Away** - Ram enemies away from objectives
2. **Create Space** - Ram to create distance for ranged attacks
3. **Block Paths** - Push terrain to block enemy movement
4. **Protect Objectives** - Ram enemies away from capture points

### Tactical Considerations
1. **Positioning** - Position yourself for effective rams
2. **Ledge Awareness** - Look for ledges to push enemies off
3. **Terrain Setup** - Use terrain for indirect rams
4. **Movement Economy** - Ramming costs 1 AP (same as normal movement)

## BALANCE CONSIDERATIONS

### Why This Works
1. **Hands-Off Rule** - Prevents manipulation and cheating
2. **Costs AP** - Ramming costs 1 AP (same as normal movement)
3. **No Direct Damage** - Ramming doesn't deal damage (only fall damage)
4. **Counterplay** - Enemies can avoid being near ledges
5. **Physics-Based** - Natural collisions keep it fair

### Counterplay Options
1. **Avoid Ledges** - Stay away from high ground edges
2. **Stable Positioning** - Position on stable ground
3. **Spread Out** - Don't cluster (prevents multi-ram)
4. **Ram First** - Ram enemies before they ram you
