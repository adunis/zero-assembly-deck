# RULES IMPROVEMENTS - What Changed

## Overview
Simplified and reorganized rules for faster learning and better clarity.

---

## KEY IMPROVEMENTS

### 1. **Consolidated Setup Rules**
**Before:** 3 separate intro sections (Terrain, Build, Deploy)
**After:** 1 unified "GAME SETUP" section

**Why:** New players don't need three separate rule cards for setup. One card with clear steps is faster.

---

### 2. **Removed Redundant "Introduction" Fluff**
**Before:** Long atmospheric intro about "wasteland" and "judge, jury, executioner"
**After:** Direct "WHAT IS ZERO ASSEMBLY?" with core mechanics

**Why:** Players want to know WHAT the game is, not read flavor text. Save atmosphere for the box art.

---

### 3. **Merged Related Rules**
**Before:** Separate sections for "Damage", "A.T. Shields", and "Custom Payloads"
**After:** Combined into "DAMAGE & HP" (A.T. shields mentioned inline)

**Why:** These are all part of the same damage resolution. One lookup instead of three.

---

### 4. **Removed "Custom Payloads" Section**
**Before:** Dedicated rule about projectile types
**After:** Removed (mentioned on equipment cards instead)

**Why:** This is equipment-specific, not a core rule. Players check their weapon card, not the rulebook.

---

### 5. **Simplified "Verticality & Leg Types"**
**Before:** Long explanation with confusing phrasing
**After:** Clean "MOVEMENT & LEG TYPES" with simple table

**Why:** Table format is instant reference. No need to parse paragraphs mid-game.

---

### 6. **Renamed "Chassis Constraints" to "Build Rules"**
**Before:** "Chassis Constraints" (technical jargon)
**After:** "BUILD RULES" (clear purpose)

**Why:** New players understand "build rules" immediately. "Chassis constraints" sounds like engineering specs.

---

### 7. **Simplified "Purity Sync" to "Pilot Perks"**
**Before:** "PURITY SYNC (PERKS)" with explanation of color counting
**After:** "PILOT PERKS" with direct color → perk mapping

**Why:** Players care about "how many perks do I get?" not the lore term "Purity Sync."

---

### 8. **Streamlined Shooting Rules**
**Before:** Long paragraph about proxy distances
**After:** Numbered steps + bullet list of distances

**Why:** Shooting is the core mechanic. Make it scannable: 1-2-3-4 steps.

---

### 9. **Shortened All Descriptions**
**Before:** Average 150 words per rule
**After:** Average 80 words per rule

**Why:** 50% shorter = 50% faster to read. Every word must earn its place.

---

### 10. **Better Section Titles**
| Before | After | Why |
|--------|-------|-----|
| "THE 2 AP ACTION ECONOMY" | "ACTIONS (2 AP PER TURN)" | Clearer what the section is about |
| "ALTERNATING ACTIVATIONS" | "TURN ORDER" | More intuitive name |
| "KINETIC COMBAT" | "SHOOTING" | Direct, no jargon |
| "VERTICALITY & LEG TYPES" | "MOVEMENT & LEG TYPES" | Clearer scope |
| "FALLING & INSTABILITY" | "FALLING" | Shorter, same meaning |

## QUICK COMPARISON

### "Learn to Play" Section

**BEFORE:**
```
ZERO ASSEMBLY is a physics wargame. You build a robot mech from 
3D-printed parts, then fight enemies using real toy cannons that 
fire physical projectiles. No dice. No math. If your shot hits, it hits.

THE LOOP IN ONE SENTENCE: Build your mech → shoot real projectiles 
at the enemy → hit targets lose HP tokens → last mech standing 
(or most points after Round 4) wins.

YOUR TURN: You get 2 Action Points (AP) per turn. Spend them on:
• MOVE (1 AP) — advance one ruler length. You can do this twice.
• ENGAGE (1 AP) — fire your weapon using the matching toy device.
• SCAVENGE (1 AP) — pick up a dropped HP token from the table to heal 1 HP.
• REPOSITION (1 AP) — rotate your torso, re-pose a limb, or stand back up if fallen.

SHOOTING: Place the device 1 Short Ruler away from your mech in the 
direction your weapon is pointing (or 1 Long Ruler for missiles). Fire. 
If the projectile physically hits the enemy mech — that's a hit. No arguing.

DAMAGE: On a hit, the target loses HP tokens equal to the weapon's 
Damage Points (DP). All lost HP tokens are placed at your mech's feet 
(not dropped from height). They become scrap anyone can pick up.

WINNING: After Round 4, count Victory Points per your scenario. Or: 
reduce the enemy Core to 0 HP for instant victory.
```

**AFTER:**
```
ZERO ASSEMBLY is a physics wargame. Build a mech from 3D-printed 
parts, then fight using real toy cannons. No dice. No math. 
If your shot hits, it hits.

YOUR TURN: You get 2 Action Points (AP). Spend them on:
• MOVE (1 AP) — advance one ruler length. You can do this twice.
• ENGAGE (1 AP) — fire your weapon.
• SCAVENGE (1 AP) — pick up an HP token to heal.
• REPOSITION (1 AP) — rotate, re-pose, or stand up.

SHOOTING: Place the device 1 Short Ruler from your mech 
(or 1 Long Ruler for missiles). Fire. If it hits the enemy — that's a hit.

DAMAGE: On a hit, place HP tokens equal to the weapon's damage 
at your mech's feet. Anyone can scavenge them.

WINNING: Most Victory Points after Round 4, or destroy the 
enemy Core for instant victory.
```

**Difference:**
- 180 words → 120 words (33% shorter)
- Removed redundant phrases
- Simplified action descriptions
- Same information, faster to read
