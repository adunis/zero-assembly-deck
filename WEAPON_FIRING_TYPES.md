# 🎯 WEAPON FIRING TYPES
## Direct Fire, Indirect Fire, and Lock-On Systems

**Status:** Fully integrated
**Purpose:** Create tactical variety through different weapon mechanics

---

## 📋 OVERVIEW

Zero Assembly features **three distinct weapon types**, each with unique mechanics:

1. **DIRECT FIRE** - Straight-line weapons (rifles, lasers, cannons)
2. **INDIRECT FIRE** - Arc-trajectory weapons (mortars, howitzers)
3. **LOCK-ON** - Guided missiles with tracking systems

Each type has strengths and weaknesses, creating tactical decisions about weapon loadouts.

---

## 🎯 DIRECT FIRE WEAPONS

### Mechanics

**Requirements:**
- Must have line of sight to target
- Affected by cover modifiers
- Standard to-hit roll (2D6, need 7+)

**Procedure:**
1. Check line of sight (must see target)
2. Measure range
3. Roll 2D6 + modifiers
4. Apply cover penalties
5. If hit: Deal DP + Finger Flick

**Modifiers:**
- Range: Short +1, Medium 0, Long -1
- Cover: Partial -1, Heavy -2
- Movement: Target moved -1, Shooter moved -1

### Strengths

✅ **Most accurate** - No inherent penalty
✅ **Immediate** - Fire in one turn
✅ **Flexible** - Can shoot and move
✅ **Reliable** - Standard mechanics

### Weaknesses

❌ **Requires LoS** - Can't shoot through terrain
❌ **Affected by cover** - Enemies can hide
❌ **Predictable** - Standard counters apply

### Examples

**Light Direct Fire:**
- G-36 Micro-Rifle (1 DP, Medium range)
- FAL-X Autocannon (1 DP, Medium range)
- Paralyzer EMP Beam (1 DP, Medium range)

**Medium Direct Fire:**
- M10 Breacher Shotgun (2 DP, Short range)
- Lancer Railgun (2 DP, Long range)
- Phalanx Gatling Array (2 DP, Medium range)

**Heavy Direct Fire:**
- Bunker Pile Driver (3 DP, Melee, auto-hit)

### Tactical Use

**Best For:**
- Aggressive players
- Mobile builds
- Close-range combat
- Flanking maneuvers

**Tactics:**
- Get close for +1 to hit
- Flank to avoid cover
- Combine with mobility
- Use multi-shot weapons

## 🏔️ INDIRECT FIRE WEAPONS

### Mechanics

**Requirements:**
- Does NOT require line of sight
- Ignores all cover (Partial and Heavy)
- -1 to hit penalty (arc trajectory)

**Procedure:**
1. Declare target (no LoS check needed!)
2. Measure range
3. Roll 2D6 + modifiers **-1 (indirect penalty)**
4. Cover modifiers do NOT apply
5. If hit: Deal DP + Finger Flick

**Modifiers:**
- Range: Short +1, Medium 0, Long -1
- **Indirect Fire: -1** (always)
- Movement: Target moved -1, Shooter moved -1
- Cover: **Ignored** (no penalty)

**Example Calculation:**
- Medium range (0) + Indirect (-1) = need 8+ to hit
- Long range (-1) + Indirect (-1) = need 9+ to hit
- Short range (+1) + Indirect (-1) = need 7+ to hit

### Strengths

✅ **No LoS required** - Shoot over terrain
✅ **Ignores cover** - Enemies can't hide
✅ **Area denial** - Control zones
✅ **Support fire** - Help teammates

### Weaknesses

❌ **Less accurate** - -1 to hit penalty
❌ **Predictable arc** - Enemies know you're shooting blind
❌ **Still affected by range** - Long range is hard

### Examples

**Light Indirect Fire:**
- Brimstone Micro-Missile Pod (1 DP × 2 shots, Long range)

**Medium Indirect Fire:**
- MK-20 Rhino Heavy Cannon (2 DP, Medium range)

**Heavy Indirect Fire:**
- Storm Shadow Demolition Pod (3 DP, Long range, 2 flicks)

### Tactical Use

**Best For:**
- Support players
- Static builds
- Area control
- Punishing campers

**Tactics:**
- Target enemies in heavy cover
- Shoot over buildings
- Force enemies to move
- Combine with spotters (teammates with LoS)

**Synergies:**
- Pair with long-range sensors
- Use with defensive builds
- Combine with area denial weapons
- Support aggressive teammates

## 🎯 LOCK-ON WEAPONS

### Mechanics

**Requirements:**
- Two-turn process: LOCK then FIRE
- Requires LoS to LOCK (not to FIRE)
- Lock breaks if shooter moves (unless special ability)

**TURN 1 - LOCK Procedure:**
1. ENGAGE (1 AP) to initiate lock
2. Check line of sight (must see target)
3. Measure range (must be within weapon range)
4. Roll 2D6 + modifiers (need 7+)
5. If successful: Place LOCK token on target
6. If failed: No lock, can try again next turn

**TURN 2 - FIRE Procedure:**
1. ENGAGE (1 AP) to fire at locked target
2. **No LoS check** (missile tracks target!)
3. **Ignores all cover** (missile maneuvers)
4. **Auto-hit** (no roll needed!)
5. Deal DP + Finger Flick
6. Remove LOCK token

**Lock Restrictions:**
- Can only maintain 1 lock at a time (per weapon)
- Lock breaks if shooter moves (unless special ability)
- Lock breaks if target moves out of range
- Lock persists if target moves but stays in range
- Must use same weapon to fire as you used to lock

### Strengths

✅ **Guaranteed hit** - Auto-hit when fired
✅ **Ignores cover** - Missile maneuvers around obstacles
✅ **No LoS to fire** - Target can hide after lock
✅ **Psychological** - Forces enemy to react

### Weaknesses

❌ **Two turns** - Slow to execute
❌ **Telegraphed** - Enemy knows you're locking
❌ **Breaks on movement** - Must stay still (usually)
❌ **Requires LoS to lock** - Initial lock needs sight

### Examples

**Standard Lock-On:**
- Starstreak Missile Rack (2 DP, Long range)
- Javelin Lock-On Launcher (3 DP, Long range, 2 flicks)

**Advanced Lock-On:**
- Seeker Smart Missile (2 DP, Medium range, lock persists when moving)
- Swarm Multi-Lock System (1 DP per target, can lock 2 targets)

### Tactical Use

**Best For:**
- Patient players
- Defensive builds
- Anti-tank roles
- Punishing static enemies

**Tactics:**

**1. Lock-and-Hold:**
- Turn 1: LOCK target
- Turn 2: FIRE (guaranteed hit)
- Best against static enemies

**2. Lock-and-Reposition:**
- Turn 1: LOCK target
- Turn 2: MOVE + FIRE (if using Seeker Smart Missile)
- Mobile lock-on

**3. Lock-and-Pressure:**
- Turn 1: LOCK target
- Enemy must move or take guaranteed hit
- Forces enemy to waste AP

**4. Multi-Lock:**
- Turn 1: LOCK 2 targets (Swarm system)
- Turn 2: FIRE at both (2 guaranteed hits)
- Area control

**Synergies:**
- Pair with defensive shields (recharge while locking)
- Use with long-range builds (lock from safety)
- Combine with area denial (force enemies into locks)
- Support aggressive teammates (lock their targets)

## 🎮 TACTICAL IMPLICATIONS

### Weapon Loadout Decisions

**All Direct Fire:**
- Maximum flexibility
- Best for mobile builds
- Requires good positioning
- Vulnerable to cover

**All Indirect Fire:**
- Maximum area control
- Best for static builds
- Less accurate
- Forces enemies to move

**All Lock-On:**
- Maximum guaranteed damage
- Best for defensive builds
- Slow to execute
- Requires patience

**Mixed Loadout:**
- Tactical flexibility
- Adapt to situations
- Cover all scenarios
- Most versatile

### Counter-Play

**Against Direct Fire:**
- Use cover (Partial -1, Heavy -2)
- Break line of sight
- Stay at long range
- Keep moving

**Against Indirect Fire:**
- Close the distance (harder to hit at short range with -1 penalty)
- Keep moving (-1 to hit)
- Pressure the shooter (force them to move)
- Use shields (absorb hits)

**Against Lock-On:**
- **Move immediately** when locked (break lock)
- Stay out of LoS (can't lock)
- Pressure the shooter (force them to move, breaks lock)
- Use fast builds (move out of range)

## 💡 ADVANCED TACTICS

### Direct Fire Tactics

**1. Flanking:**
- Move to side/rear of enemy
- Avoid their cover
- Get clean shot

**2. Close Assault:**
- Rush to Short range (+1 to hit)
- Use shotguns/close weapons
- Overwhelm with accuracy

**3. Suppression:**
- Multi-shot weapons
- Force enemies to stay in cover
- Control movement

### Indirect Fire Tactics

**1. Blind Fire:**
- Shoot at last known position
- Force enemies to relocate
- Area denial

**2. Spotter Support:**
- Teammate provides LoS
- You provide fire support
- Coordinate attacks

**3. Static Defense:**
- Hold objective
- Shoot over cover
- Punish attackers

### Lock-On Tactics

**1. Lock-and-Wait:**
- Lock target
- Wait for them to move into bad position
- Fire when optimal

**2. Lock-and-Threaten:**
- Lock target
- Force them to move
- Teammate shoots them while moving

**3. Multi-Lock Pressure:**
- Lock multiple targets (Swarm)
- Force multiple enemies to react
- Control battlefield

**4. Mobile Lock (Seeker):**
- Lock target
- Reposition while maintaining lock
- Fire from better position

## 📈 PROBABILITY ANALYSIS

### Direct Fire Hit Chances

| Scenario | Modifiers | Target | Chance |
|----------|-----------|--------|--------|
| Short, no cover | +1 | 6+ | 72% |
| Medium, no cover | 0 | 7+ | 58% |
| Medium, partial cover | -1 | 8+ | 42% |
| Long, heavy cover | -3 | 10+ | 17% |

### Indirect Fire Hit Chances

| Scenario | Modifiers | Target | Chance |
|----------|-----------|--------|--------|
| Short, any cover | +1, -1 | 7+ | 58% |
| Medium, any cover | 0, -1 | 8+ | 42% |
| Long, any cover | -1, -1 | 9+ | 28% |

**Note:** Indirect Fire ignores cover but has -1 penalty, so it's often similar to shooting at partial cover.

### Lock-On Success

| Phase | Roll Required | Chance | Result |
|-------|---------------|--------|--------|
| LOCK | 7+ | 58% | Lock established |
| FIRE | Auto-hit | 100% | Guaranteed damage |
| **Total** | - | **58%** | Over 2 turns |

**Analysis:** Lock-On has 58% chance to establish lock, but once locked, 100% hit rate. Effective hit rate over 2 turns is 58%, but damage is guaranteed if lock succeeds.
