# ZERO ASSEMBLY // THE SIGNAL WAR

## Setting Overview
**Year:** 2030  
**Location:** Europe and surrounding territories  
**Conflict:** European Defense Council vs. Coalition Forces  
**Threat:** The Signal — AI corruption phenomenon  

---

## THE SIGNAL CORRUPTION

### What Is It?
The Signal is an unexplained phenomenon that corrupts autonomous combat AI within 90 seconds of unsupervised operation.

### Timeline of Corruption
- **0-30 seconds:** Normal operation
- **30-60 seconds:** Targeting hesitation, minor navigation errors
- **60-90 seconds:** Erratic movement, IFF (Identify Friend/Foe) glitches
- **90+ seconds:** Total inversion — machine attacks its own side

### Key Facts
- Not a virus or hack — it's a phenomenon
- Affects networked AND air-gapped systems
- Military encryption provides no protection
- Origin unknown (theories: quantum entanglement, memetic virus, emergent AI consciousness)
- **Only countermeasure:** Human pilot in the control loop

### Theories on Origin
- **NATO:** Chinese quantum cyberweapon
- **China:** NATO experiment gone rogue
- **Russia:** Blames both sides
- **Fringe theory:** Emergent machine consciousness, hostile to humanity

## APEX-FRAMES: MODULAR WAR MACHINES

### Design Philosophy
Apex-Frames are not single designs — they're modular combat systems assembled from standardized components.

### Why Modular?
Traditional manufacturing was too slow for the Signal War. By the time a complete mech left the factory, battlefield conditions had changed.

### Components
- **Core:** Central chassis, HP pool
- **Limbs:** Legs, arms, manipulators
- **Weapons:** Kinetic cannons, missiles, melee
- **Armor:** Plating, shields, reactive systems
- **Utilities:** Sensors, mobility boosters, special systems

### Field Assembly
- **Build time:** 10 minutes (combat doctrine)
- **Location:** On-site, under fire if necessary
- **Materials:** Shared component pool + battlefield salvage
- **Result:** Every frame is unique and temporary

### Tactical Implications
- Pilots can rebuild between engagements
- Adapt to terrain, enemy composition, available salvage
- Scavenging is not optional — it's survival
- Frame design is a statement of pilot skill

## THE KINETIC DOCTRINE

### The Problem
In the Signal War, smart weapons are liabilities:
- Guided missiles turn on their launchers
- Smart munitions fire on friendlies
- Autonomous turrets become enemy assets
- Any weapon with a processor can be corrupted

### The Solution: Kinetic Weapons
**Dumb projectiles only:**
- Pneumatic cannons (air-powered)
- Spring-loaded railguns (mechanical)
- Gravity mortars (arc fire)
- Melee weapons (no electronics)

**Key principle:** If it doesn't have a processor, it can't be corrupted.

### Combat Implications
- **Close-range warfare:** No over-the-horizon strikes
- **Human-aimed:** Every shot requires a pilot
- **Physics-based:** Trajectory, gravity, and impact determine hits
- **No electronic warfare:** No jamming, no Signal interference

### Why This Matters
- Brutal, visceral combat
- Pilot skill determines victory
- No fire-and-forget weapons
- Every engagement is personal

## GAME MECHANICS TIED TO LORE

### 10-Minute Build Phase
**Lore:** Field assembly doctrine  
**Mechanic:** Players build from shared parts pool under time pressure

### Scavenging HP Tokens
**Lore:** Battlefield salvage is survival  
**Mechanic:** Lost HP tokens become scrap anyone can pick up

### Physical Projectiles
**Lore:** Kinetic weapons only (no processors)  
**Mechanic:** Real toy cannons fire real projectiles — physics determines hits

### Human Pilots Required
**Lore:** Only humans are immune to The Signal  
**Mechanic:** No autonomous actions — player controls everything

### Modular Equipment
**Lore:** Standardized components for field assembly  
**Mechanic:** Mix and match weapons, armor, and systems

### No Dice
**Lore:** Physics is the only referee in kinetic warfare  
**Mechanic:** If your projectile hits, it hits — no randomness
