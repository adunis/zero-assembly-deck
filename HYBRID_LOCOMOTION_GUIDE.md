# Hybrid Locomotion Guide

## 🤖 Multiple Leg Types on One Mech

Your mech can have **multiple leg types installed simultaneously**! This allows for tactical flexibility — use wheels for speed on flat terrain, then switch to legs for climbing buildings.

---

## ✅ Qualification Requirements

To use a specific movement type, your mech must meet the **minimum requirements**:

### 🛞 WHEELS
**Requirement:** At least **2 wheels** touching the ground  
**Movement:** 1 Long Ruler per MOVE action  
**Special:** Ground-locked (cannot climb)  
**Best For:** Speed on flat terrain

### 🚜 TREADS
**Requirement:** At least **2 treads** touching the ground (on the tread side, not the wheel side)  
**Movement:** 1 Short Ruler per MOVE action  
**Special:** Ground-locked (cannot climb)  
**Best For:** Stability and balance

### 🕷️ SPIDER
**Requirement:** At least **3 spider legs** touching the ground  
**Movement:** 1 Short Ruler per MOVE action  
**Special:** Can climb (1 AP to reach any height)  
**Best For:** Climbing buildings, vertical mobility

### 🦿 BIPED
**Requirement:** Exactly **2 legs** touching the ground  
**Movement:** 1 Short Ruler per MOVE action  
**Special:** Can climb (1 AP) + automatically step over obstacles <5cm  
**Best For:** Versatility and obstacle clearance

---

## 🎮 How Hybrid Locomotion Works

### During the Build Phase:
1. Install any combination of leg types you want
2. Make sure your mech stands on its own
3. Count which leg types you qualify for

### During Movement:
1. Spend 1 AP to MOVE
2. **Choose** which leg type to use (if you qualify for multiple)
3. Move the corresponding distance
4. You can switch leg types on your next MOVE action

## 💡 Example Hybrid Builds

### Example 1: Speed Demon
**Build:** 4 wheels + 2 legs  
**Qualifies For:**
- ✅ Wheels (4 wheels ≥ 2 required)
- ✅ Biped (2 legs = 2 required)

**Tactics:**
- Use **Wheels** (Long Ruler) on flat terrain for maximum speed
- Switch to **Biped** (Short Ruler) when you need to climb buildings
- Biped mode lets you step over small obstacles automatically

**Qualifies For:**

## 🎯 Strategic Considerations

### Redundancy
If you have multiple leg types, losing some legs to damage doesn't immobilize you:
- **6 spider legs:** Can lose 3 and still qualify for spider movement
- **4 wheels + 2 legs:** Can lose 2 wheels and still qualify for wheels, or switch to biped

### Tactical Flexibility
Switch movement types mid-activation:
- **Turn 1:** MOVE using Wheels (Long Ruler) - rush forward
- **Turn 1:** MOVE using Biped (Short Ruler) - climb building
- **Result:** You moved fast, then climbed in the same activation!

### Build Tradeoffs
More leg types = more flexibility, but:
- Takes up more attachment points
- More weight (may affect balance)
- More complex builds (harder to assemble in 10 minutes)

## ⚠️ Important Notes

1. **Treads:** Must touch the ground on the **tread side**, not the wheel side
2. **Biped:** Requires **exactly 2 legs**, not more (3+ legs = spider movement)
3. **Choosing:** You choose which movement type to use **each time you MOVE**
4. **Switching:** You can switch between movement types during the same activation
5. **Climbing:** Only Spider and Biped can climb (1 AP to reach any height)

---

## 🏗️ Build Phase Tips

1. **Plan Your Mobility:** Decide if you want speed (wheels), stability (treads), or climbing (spider/biped)
2. **Mix and Match:** Combine leg types for tactical flexibility
3. **Test Stability:** Make sure your mech stands with all leg types touching the ground
4. **Count Legs:** Verify you meet the minimum requirements for each movement type
5. **Balance Weight:** More legs = more weight, may affect balance

## 🎮 In-Game Example

**Your Mech:** 4 wheels + 2 legs (qualifies for Wheels and Biped)

**Situation:** You're on flat ground, enemy is on a rooftop

**Your Activation (2 AP):**
1. **MOVE (1 AP) using Wheels:** Move 1 Long Ruler forward (fast approach)
2. **MOVE (1 AP) using Biped:** Climb the building (1 AP = reach rooftop)

**Result:** You moved fast across open ground, then climbed to engage the enemy!
